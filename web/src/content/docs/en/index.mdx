## Protocol overview

**Hongbao** (红包) is the Mandarin word for "red packet." The Hongbao protocol brings the red-packet paradigm on-chain: a physical card *is* a red packet — the secure element inside generates a secp256k1 private key at the factory and seals it permanently. Hand the card to someone, and control of those assets transfers to them completely and irreversibly.

**The protocol is built from four components:**

| Component | Role | Form |
|---|---|---|
| **Hardware signing device** (the card) | One-time secp256k1 signing device | Designed, manufactured, and sold by Hongbao |
| **Client** (Web / Android / iOS) | Guides the cardholder through claiming | Provided by Hongbao |
| **Standard contract** | On-chain custody — binds the card address, releases funds after verifying the signature | Open-source; anyone can deploy / fork / extend |
| **Relayer** | Pays gas to submit the withdraw transaction | A non-trusted role — any EOA can serve |

**The on-chain layer is fully open** — wallets, exchanges, and dapps can all integrate or extend against this spec.

**Supported chains:** secp256k1 is the de facto universal curve — Bitcoin, Ethereum and all EVM chains, Solana, Sui, Aptos, Tron, Cosmos, and more. Live today: full EVM family (ERC20 + ERC721). Others available on demand.
