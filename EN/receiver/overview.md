# Before You Claim

**You've got a Hongbao card. Scan it, give it a press, and the assets arrive in seconds.**

This card holds on-chain assets — maybe stablecoins (USDT / USDC), maybe popular tokens (ETH / BNB / SOL), maybe an NFT, or maybe any ERC20 / ERC721 or even non-standard token the issuer chose to mint. What it is and how much is inside is up to the issuer, and the App shows it to you the moment it connects to the card.

It isn't a voucher, it isn't points, and it isn't an ordinary IOU. The card itself is the on-chain native asset. Scan the QR code, press the button on the card, and within seconds it lands in the wallet you name. There's no gas to pay, and you don't need to know a thing about blockchain.

---

## What it is

On the outside, it's about the size of a bank card and a little thicker than a credit card. Inside, you'll find:

- A secure element (where the private key hides)
- A button
- Two indicator lights (red and green)
- A single-use battery
- A QR code (printed on the card face)

At its core, it's a single-use on-chain asset credential — like cash, whoever holds it owns the assets:

- Inside is a private key, randomly generated by the chip itself at the factory and then permanently sealed in hardware. No one can ever export it — not us, not the issuer, not the manufacturer, not even you.
- That private key corresponds to a batch of assets locked on-chain; the issuer locked the funds away before the card ever reached you.
- The card can only sign once. The moment you press the button to authorize, the assets move into the wallet you named. This is a design promise, not a technical limitation: every card guarantees one definite transfer of ownership.

## Plain card vs. task card

The card you've got is one of two kinds, and the App will tell you which once it connects:

- Plain card: scan, press once, and the full amount arrives.
- Task card: scan and press once to claim the basic amount, which also locks in your recipient address; the rest unlocks as you complete tasks — like following the issuer, resharing a post, joining a group, or verifying on-chain activity. Each task you finish automatically sends the matching amount to the address you just locked in. You won't need to touch the card again to unlock tasks — just follow the prompts in the App. Anything you leave undone goes back to the issuer once the card expires.

## What you'll need

Almost nothing. Open the Hongbao App ([Android APK](https://github.com/hongbao-labs/HB_Android/releases/download/v1.0.4/hongbao-v1.0.4-release.apk), with iOS and Web coming soon) and follow the prompts.

If you don't have a crypto wallet or exchange account yet, no worries. The App will first take you to a mainstream wallet or exchange to sign up and get a recipient address, then bring you right back to Hongbao to finish claiming.


> Your wallet's private key and account always stay with the wallet or exchange itself — Hongbao doesn't create, custody, or touch your private key. We only guide you through signing up and handle the payout.

## Why it's safe

Four independent defenses, each one enough on its own to stop assets from being lost:

1. The private key never leaves the chip. It's been sealed in hardware since the day it was made — we keep no backup and have no way to recover it.
2. Authorization requires a physical button press. A Bluetooth hijack leads nowhere: no button press, no signature.
3. The recipient address is written into the signature. The address you enter is part of what gets signed, so any middleman who tries to change it breaks the signature, and the contract rejects it outright.
4. The card can only sign once. A claimed card becomes a keepsake — even if someone steals it, there's nothing left inside to take.

The smart contract holding the assets has no owner, no pause button, and no upgrade mechanism, so even if the issuer had second thoughts, they couldn't pull the assets back (until the expiry time arrives). The code is fully public and open to independent audit.
