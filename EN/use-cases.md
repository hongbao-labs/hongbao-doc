# Hongbao — The 场景 Library (Use Cases)

24 ways a physical card carrying on-chain rewards beats a bare wallet address. Grouped by buyer motion. Each entry: **who buys · the pain · why Hongbao wins · the one-line pitch.**

The thread running through all of them is the same primitive — **a bearer object that proves value on tap and releases it non-custodially on press** — applied to a different moment where crypto has to cross from the screen into the physical world.

> New here? Start with the [overview](README.md) for what Hongbao is and how a card works, then come back for where it fits.

## Tier 1 — Growth & Acquisition

### 1. Sybil-proof airdrops
- **Buyer:** token projects, L1/L2 foundations, protocol growth leads.
- **Pain:** a large share of airdrop claimants are sybil farms; budgets reach bots, not users.
- **Why Hongbao:** you can't farm what you have to physically pick up. Every claim is a human who held a card.
- **Pitch:** *"Airdrop to hands, not addresses."*

### 2. IRL events & conferences
- **Buyer:** project marketing teams exhibiting at Token2049, ETHDenver, Consensus, regional meetups.
- **Pain:** booth swag is forgotten by the elevator; "scan this QR to join" converts terribly and yields no attribution.
- **Why Hongbao:** a card with real money in it gets kept, claimed, and tied to a wallet — instant on-chain attribution from a handshake.
- **Pitch:** *"Turn a booth visit into an on-chain user."*

### 3. Quests & task campaigns
- **Buyer:** projects already running Galxe/Layer3/Zealy campaigns.
- **Pain:** quest completers are mercenary wallets that vanish after the reward.
- **Why Hongbao:** the basic amount is claimed on scan; task amounts unlock as real actions complete — and the human is verified by the card, not by a captcha.
- **Pitch:** *"Quests that pay a person, not a script."*

### 4. Loyalty & retention programs
- **Buyer:** exchanges, wallets, consumer-crypto apps.
- **Pain:** points programs are abstract; users don't feel rewarded.
- **Why Hongbao:** a physical reward card is a tangible loyalty token customers carry and remember.
- **Pitch:** *"A loyalty reward you can hold."*

### 5. Ambassador & KOL distribution kits
- **Buyer:** projects with ambassador / creator programs.
- **Pain:** giving KOLs a "code to share" is untrackable and easily abused.
- **Why Hongbao:** hand each ambassador a stack of pre-loaded cards to give out IRL; every claim is attributed back to that ambassador.
- **Pitch:** *"Give your ambassadors something real to give away."*

### 6. Referral programs
- **Buyer:** any app with a referral loop.
- **Pain:** referral fraud and self-referral farming.
- **Why Hongbao:** a physical referral card must change hands between two people.
- **Pitch:** *"Referrals you can't fake."*

### 7. Testnet → mainnet conversion
- **Buyer:** protocols graduating from testnet.
- **Pain:** rewarding genuine testnet contributors without re-airdropping to farmers.
- **Why Hongbao:** mail or hand cards to known contributors; the reward lands in their real wallet on claim.
- **Pitch:** *"Reward the testers who were actually people."*

## Tier 2 — Commerce & Exchange

### 8. Retail crypto gift cards
- **Buyer:** retailers, gift-card distributors, crypto on-ramps.
- **Pain:** buying crypto as a gift is technical and scary for normal people.
- **Why Hongbao:** packaging covers the QR — a shopper verifies the balance but can't claim until purchased; the recipient claims with no app and no seed phrase.
- **Pitch:** *"Crypto you can buy at a counter and gift by hand."*

### 9. Crypto vouchers & coupons
- **Buyer:** merchants, brands running token-denominated promos.
- **Pain:** digital codes are leaked, scraped, and bulk-redeemed.
- **Why Hongbao:** a physical voucher with on-chain value, claimable once.
- **Pitch:** *"A coupon that's worth real on-chain money — and can only be spent once."*

### 10. Trade-show lead generation
- **Buyer:** B2B crypto vendors (infra, custody, tooling).
- **Pain:** badge scans are noise; no qualified intent.
- **Why Hongbao:** "tap to verify, claim to your work wallet" — the claim *is* a qualified, wallet-identified lead.
- **Pitch:** *"Your lead capture pays the lead."*

## Tier 3 — Institutional, Payouts & Real-World

### 11. AI-agent platforms (Base / Virtuals)
- **Buyer:** agent-launch platforms, fair-launch communities.
- **Pain:** bot-vs-human is existential for fair launches and agent onboarding.
- **Why Hongbao:** onboard physically-verified humans for agent launches and allocations.
- **Pitch:** *"Proof-of-human onboarding for the agent economy."*

### 12. Hackathon prizes
- **Buyer:** hackathon organizers, ecosystem dev-rel.
- **Pain:** collecting winners' wallet addresses and processing payouts is slow and error-prone.
- **Why Hongbao:** hand the winner a card on stage; they claim to their own wallet instantly.
- **Pitch:** *"Pay the winner on stage, no spreadsheet."*

### 13. DAO contributor & bounty payouts
- **Buyer:** DAOs, grant programs.
- **Pain:** multisig payout friction and address-collection overhead for small bounties.
- **Why Hongbao:** pre-load cards for bounties; contributors claim when work lands.
- **Pitch:** *"Bounties you can hand out at the meetup."*

### 14. Employee crypto bonuses & onboarding
- **Buyer:** Web3-native companies.
- **Pain:** onboarding new hires into token comp is technical and impersonal.
- **Why Hongbao:** a welcome card with a token bonus — claimed to the employee's wallet, no IT ticket.
- **Pitch:** *"Onboard a teammate with crypto in their hand."*

### 15. Conference speaker & VIP gifts
- **Buyer:** event organizers, sponsors.
- **Pain:** VIP gifting in crypto is awkward and untracked.
- **Why Hongbao:** a premium card with a meaningful reward for speakers/VIPs.
- **Pitch:** *"The VIP gift that's actually worth something on-chain."*

### 16. Charity & disaster-relief disbursement
- **Buyer:** NGOs, relief organizations.
- **Pain:** distributing aid to people without bank access or a smartphone on hand.
- **Why Hongbao:** bearer cards work with no app to receive; every disbursement is on-chain auditable and the unclaimed remainder is reclaimable after expiry.
- **Pitch:** *"Auditable aid you can put directly in someone's hand."*

## Tier 4 — Culture, Consumer & Frontier

### 17. Tipping & creator economy
- **Buyer:** creator platforms, fan communities.
- **Pain:** tipping crypto to creators is clunky.
- **Why Hongbao:** physical tip cards fans can hand to creators at events.
- **Pitch:** *"Tip your favorite creator something real."*

### 18. Gaming & esports prizes
- **Buyer:** tournament organizers, game studios.
- **Pain:** prize payout logistics and proving winners are real players.
- **Why Hongbao:** reward cards handed to winners at LAN events and tournaments.
- **Pitch:** *"Prize pools that pay out in your hand."*

### 19. Memecoin community drops
- **Buyer:** memecoin teams, community leads.
- **Pain:** memecoin energy is online; meetups have nothing physical.
- **Why Hongbao:** hand out branded "bags" at meetups — pure cultural artifact + real value.
- **Pitch:** *"The physical bag for your community."*

### 20. NFT mint allowlist & phygital
- **Buyer:** NFT projects, phygital brands.
- **Pain:** allowlist distribution and connecting physical goods to on-chain claims.
- **Why Hongbao:** a card unlocks both tokens and a mint/claim — the physical key to a digital asset.
- **Pitch:** *"One card, one mint, one human."*

### 21. Stablecoin gifting & remittance
- **Buyer:** stablecoin apps, diaspora communities.
- **Pain:** sending a small stablecoin gift cross-border to a non-technical recipient.
- **Why Hongbao:** mail a card; recipient claims with no onboarding.
- **Pitch:** *"Send dollars by mail, on-chain."*

### 22. Brand × crypto co-marketing
- **Buyer:** consumer brands experimenting with web3, exchanges doing brand deals.
- **Pain:** brands want a web3 hook that isn't intimidating to mainstream customers.
- **Why Hongbao:** bundle a co-branded reward card with a product or campaign.
- **Pitch:** *"Put a little crypto in your brand's pocket."*

### 23. Digital 红包 — holiday & occasion gifting
- **Buyer:** consumer apps, exchanges in Asian markets, individuals.
- **Pain:** the cultural red-envelope tradition has no good crypto-native, giftable, physical form.
- **Why Hongbao:** *this is literally what the name means* — a modern, on-chain red envelope you give by hand at New Year, weddings, birthdays.
- **Pitch:** *"The red envelope, on-chain. 拥抱好运。"*

### 24. Proof-of-attendance with money attached
- **Buyer:** event organizers, communities running POAP-style programs.
- **Pain:** POAPs prove you showed up but carry no value and are trivially farmed.
- **Why Hongbao:** combine attendance proof with a real claimable reward, gated by physical presence.
- **Pitch:** *"POAP, but it pays — and only if you were really there."*
